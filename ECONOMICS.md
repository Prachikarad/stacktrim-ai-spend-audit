# Unit Economics

## Assumptions

- A completed high-intent audit lead is worth more than a newsletter signup because it includes tool stack, spend level, and savings estimate.
- Credex earns revenue when a company buys discounted AI credits or starts a procurement conversation.
- Early conversion rates are rough and should be replaced with measured funnel data.

## Lead Value Estimate

If 100 audits produce:

- 15 email captures.
- 4 high-savings leads above $500/month.
- 1 consultation booked.
- 0.25 credit purchases.

And an average successful credit purchase produces $1,500 gross profit, then each audit is worth:

`0.25 * $1,500 / 100 = $3.75`

Each captured email is worth:

`0.25 * $1,500 / 15 = $25`

## CAC By Channel

| Channel | Expected cost | Notes |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Founder community replies | $0 cash, high time cost | Best early channel because pain is already visible |
| Partner/newsletter swaps | $50-$200 effective | Works if targeted to devtool buyers |
| Search ads on "AI spend" terms | $5-$20 CPC | Needs tight landing copy and immediate audit value |
| Broad social ads | $50+ per useful lead | Likely too unfocused for MVP |

## Profitability Threshold

If a booked consultation is worth $375 in expected gross profit, and 8% of captured emails book a consult, then a captured email can support roughly `$30 CAC`. That means free/community distribution is attractive early, while paid search needs strong targeting.

## Path To $1M ARR Impact In 6 Months

The tool would need to generate roughly 70 successful $1,500 gross-profit credit purchases per month or a smaller number of larger enterprise credit deals. That likely requires repeat distribution through Credex-owned channels, procurement partnerships, and report pages that rank for vendor comparison searches.
