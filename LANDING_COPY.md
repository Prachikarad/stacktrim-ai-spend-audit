# Landing Copy & Messaging

---

## Hero Headline (Main Page H1)

**"Audit Your AI Spend in 2 Minutes"**

Why this works:
- **Promise:** Solves a real problem (don't know how much we're spending)
- **Time anchor:** 2 minutes is specific and believable
- **Action verb:** "Audit" not "Learn" (stronger)
- **Emotional trigger:** Money pressure (spending is problem)

Alternative options tested:
- "See How Much You're Really Spending on AI Tools" (too generic)
- "Consolidate Your AI Tools & Save Thousands" (too salesy)
- "Where's Your AI Budget Going?" (too passive)

## Primary CTA Button

**"Start Your Free Audit"**

Why this works:
- **"Free"** removes friction (no credit card required)
- **"Audit"** matches the value prop (not "Calculate" or "Check")
- **Color:** Blue (#2563eb) on dark background (high contrast, accessible)
- **Size:** 48px tall, 100% mobile width (thumb-friendly)

Alternative options rejected:
- "Get Started" (too vague; could mean anything)
- "Try It Now" (sounds like marketing fluff)
- "Upload Your Tools" (asks user to work before value shown)

## FAQ Section (Below Fold)

### Q1: Do you need access to my billing account?

**A:** "No. We ask what you pay for each tool. That's it. Your billing data never leaves your computer."

Why this answer:
- **Addresses fear:** Privacy/security
- **Emphasizes simplicity:** No OAuth nonsense
- **Accurate:** Data is stored client-side until opt-in email capture

### Q2: How do you get your pricing information?

**A:** "We verify every price directly from vendor websites every week. Sources are linked in your report so you can fact-check everything."

Why this answer:
- **Defensibility:** Shows we're not making up numbers
- **Trust signal:** "Fact-check everything" invites skepticism; shows confidence
- **Transparency:** Links prove we're not guesstimating

### Q3: What if the audit shows I should consolidate tools but my team doesn't want to switch?

**A:** "That's real. The audit shows the switching cost vs. savings so you can decide. Often consolidation isn't worth it if productivity drops. Only you know your team's velocity."

Why this answer:
- **Honesty:** Admits not every recommendation is actionable
- **Empathy:** Acknowledges team dynamics matter
- **Reframes:** Positions tool as helper, not salesman

### Q4: Can I share my audit result with my team or board?

**A:** "Absolutely. Every audit generates a unique, shareable link. Your company name and email are stripped for privacy. Team members can see your spending breakdown and recommendations."

Why this answer:
- **Feature highlight:** Shareable URL is a feature, not bug
- **Privacy promise:** PII stripped
- **Collaboration signal:** Encourages org-wide adoption

### Q5: What's this "Credex" thing mentioned in some reports?

**A:** "Credex offers discounted infrastructure credits (typically 30-40% off OpenAI/Anthropic retail pricing). If your spend qualifies, we'll mention it. Zero obligation to use it. You're in control."

Why this answer:
- **Transparency:** Not hiding the partnership
- **Opt-in framing:** "Zero obligation" reduces friction
- **Value prop:** 30-40% off is meaningful
- **Honesty:** "We'll mention it" not "We'll push you to it"

## On-Page Microcopy (Small But Mighty)

| Element | Copy | Why |
|---------|------|-----|
| **Form label** | "What plan are you on?" | Specific, not "Plan type" |
| **Empty state** | "Add your first tool to get started" | Encouraging, not scary |
| **Loading state** | "Analyzing your spend..." | Shows progress |
| **Error state** | "Couldn't load that pricing data. Using our most recent snapshot." | Honest, not blame-y |
| **Success state** | "Your audit is ready to share!" | Celebration tone |
| **Email input placeholder** | "your@email.com (no spam, promise)" | Reassurance + personality |
